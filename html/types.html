Pollen Types

Common Pollen Types

Learn about different types of pollen that affect air quality and allergies, and examples of each

Tree Pollen

Common in spring, tree pollen from oak, birch, and maple trees can trigger seasonal allergies.

Birch Tree

Common in the Northeast, Upper Midwest and parts of the Atlantic. Birch pollen is abunfdant in states like Minnesota, Wisconsin,New York, and New England.

Blooms in March to May, peaking in April.

Cedar Tree

Found in the South and Southwest, especially in Texas, Oklahoma and parts of Arizona.

Bloom season is mainly December to March with a peak in January and February.

Oak Tree

Abundant in the Southeast, Midwest and Northeast.The key states that it is found include Texas, Georgia, Missouri, Pensnsylvania and New York.

Blooming typically March to June, peaking around April and May.

Grass

Peak season is late spring to early summer. Bermuda and Timothy grass are major contributors.

Fescue Grass

Found primarily in the Midwest, Mid-Atlantic and parts of Northeast.common in states such as Ohio, Pennsylvania and New York.

Blooms mainly in May to July, peaking in late spring to early summer.

Bahia Grass

Native and widespread in the Southeast and Gulf Coast states, particularly Florida, Louisiana, Alabama and Georgia.

Bloom season around April to October.

Rye Grass

Common in the Pacific Northwest,Northeast and northern Midwest.States include Washington,Oregon, New York and Michigan

Blooms mainly April ro June.

Weed Pollen

Most active in fall, ragweed is the most common weed pollen causing allergic reactions.

Ragweed

One of the most common and widespread weed pollens. Found predominantly in the Midwest, Northeast and Southeast.States with high ragweeds pollen include Illinois, Ohio, Pennsylvania, New York and Tennessee.

Blooms from August to November peaking in September.

Russian Thistle

Mostly found in the arid and semi-arid regions of the Southwest, including parts of Texas,New Mexico, Arizona and Carlifornia.

Blooming season is from August to October.

Pigweed

Common in the Midwest and Southern states, including states like Arkansas,Oklahoma, Texas and Missouri.

Blooms mainly July to October.
